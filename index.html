THE CORE SCIENCE OF SPACE SUIT ANALOGS THERMODYNAMICS // PROTOCOL

CONDUCTION

Kinetic thermal energy transfer via direct atomic collision. In our capsule setup, heat moves from internal fluids outward through solid bounding walls. Air pockets in materials like bubble wrap minimize this transfer.

CONVECTION

Heat movement via fluid circulatory displacement (gases or liquids). Warm core fluids become less dense and rise, creating currents. Sealed hardware seals and outer chambers eliminate exterior convective air currents.

RADIATION

Electromagnetic infrared emission traveling through mediums or open vacuums. Reflective Mylar foil reduces radiative heat loss by reflecting infrared radiation back inward toward the telemetry jar core.
